CASE STUDY

Ethylbenzene wastewater treatment solution

Course: Dynamics in Chemical Transport | Spring 2024
Presentation | Report

Context

In the course, Projects Practicum in Environmental Engineering, teams of four were asked to solve an environmental contamination problem given by a mock client (McCormickCEE) with the approach and expertise of a consulting firm. Our group, TBSS Inc., presented our solution to the class and shared a final report.

Problem

MCC reached out to our firm to inquire about best practices related to ethylbenzene waste produced by a new petroleum branch of their company. They sought advice on:

  1. The size of the reactor needed to treat their waste and best location along the river nearby to release the outflow
  2. Meeting the maximum contamination level and monitoring environmental impact of discharge

Our Approach

TBSS Inc. used the following methods to make a final recommendation:
  • Calculated the footprint area of a bioreactor required to treat the waste, for both location A and B
  • Determined which location is optimal in terms of river water quality by calculating the mixed biological oxygen demand (BOD) levels of the river and the effluent
  • Calculated minimum dissolved oxygen (DO) of the river

    • Final Recommendation

    1. Location A and Reactor 1 are optimal if a permit can be obtained for Location A.
    2. Both locations will result in water quality higher than the MCL.
