TERRA TABLE

Course: Furniture Design | Spring 2024
Final Design Book PDF | Showcase Video

Project Summary

I designed a side-table, called the Terra Table, in 10 weeks for a furniture design class in Spring ‘24. Despite limited prior experience with shop machinery, I had an ambitious goal to build something I would want to look at every morning: a bedside table, featuring my love for plants. My table:

  • Integrates indoor plants with the piece of furniture itself, rather than just resting on its surface in a pot
  • Utilizes only joinery and bolts (no screws or nails) and can be disassembled
  • Is entirely handmade (no CNC or other computer manufacturing tools were used)

    • Process

    1. Ideation: I was inspired by recycled glass bricks I found for free in a Chicago suburb and thought that their hollow structure would make the perfect cavity for a plant. I also envisioned the foundation being a puzzle piece of nesting components that were held together by the weight of the glass blocks.
    2. Prototyping: Figuring out how to make the interlocking grid for the foundation of my table was tricky and required lots of prototyping with paper, foam core, and scrap plywood.
    3. Mockups: I drew my table to scale in Adobe Illustrator, forming the individual pieces and then bringing them together to ensure the pieces fit together perfectly. I was also able to visualize my table better and get more organized before the construction step.
    4. Construction: I built the foundation of my table using red oak 4x4s and utilized the table saw and mill to “carve” out the shape each individual piece. I assembled the table using gold bolts and thread inserts, and finally put the glass blocks in place. I also used a water jet to cut the top off of a glass block in order to put a plant inside.

    Learnings

  • Manufacturing: When designing anything from scratch, it's super important to take the time to make precise calculations, drawings, etc. to stay organized and ensure that the building process will go smoothly.
  • Problem-solving: I encountered many challenges during the process, including difficulty with cutting my glass block, delayed shipping times, and more. It took a lot of patience and creativity to figure out how to keep moving forward under the time pressure.
  • Shop Skills: I came into the class with little to no woodworking experience and left having used nearly every machine in the shop.
  • Adobe Illustrator: This tool was pivotal for the success of my project and will be useful for sketching and mockups in the future.
