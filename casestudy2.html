CASE STUDY

Bioreactor solutions to CO2 emissions

Course: Dynamics in Chemical Transport | Spring 2024
Presentation | Report

Context

In the course, Dynamics in Chemical Transport, teams of four were asked to solve an environmental contamination problem given by a mock client (McCormick Evanston Energy) with the approach and expertise of a consulting firm. Our group, BEKS Consulting Inc., presented our solution to the class and shared a final report.

Problem

McCormick Evanston Energy (MEE) requested guidance with the emissions and environmental impact of their coal power plant, which is exceeding regulation requirements for CO2. BEKS Consulting Inc. was tasked with the following:

  1. Designing a bioreactor and algal pond system to treat CO2 waste
  2. Producing ethanol as a sellable byproduct at the same time
  3. Meeting EPA regulations for CO2 emissions
  4. Treating any groundwater contamination from ethanol byproduct

Our Approach

By treating its waste, MEE can meet EPA requirements and make some extra profit. We…
  • Recommended a length for a trickling biofilm reactor that will treat the coal plant’s waste enough to meet EPA requirements
  • Evaluated profits generated from the bioreactor's ethanol product
  • Provided an option for secondary treatment using an algae pond, and recommend what type of algae to use
  • Evaluated profits generated by the algae pond’s AST product
  • Studied the aquifer contaminated with ethanol and evaluated the retardation factor R and the hydraulic conductivity K

    • Final Recommendation

    1. Bioreactor length = 3.65 m → MEE can make $1145.90/year from ethanol
    2. MEE should implement algae pond with algae type #2
    3. Will receive stipend from EPA and make additional AST revenue for a total of $20,950/year
    4. Ethanol contamination in aquifer requires clean up.
